## Critical Injuries (Game-Changing Impact)

### Cooper Flagg — Duke (1-seed, East) — Back Injury
- **Status:** Game-time decision / Questionable
- **Injury:** Lower back strain, suffered in ACC Tournament
- **Impact Rating:** 10/10 — Flagg is 40% of Duke's ceiling
- **KenPom Adjustment:** Duke drops from #2 overall (~+29.7 AEM) to approximately #8-10 overall (~+24 AEM) without Flagg at full speed
- **Offensive Impact:** Flagg averages 21.2 PPG, 9.8 RPG, 4.6 APG. No other Duke player averages more than 14 PPG.
- **Defensive Impact:** Flagg is Duke's best help defender and rim protector. Defense loses elite switchability.
- **Bracket Implication:** **DO NOT pick Duke as champion.** The risk-reward is toxic: 38% pool ownership means winning with Duke barely matters. If he's 85% healthy, Kansas still beats them in Sweet 16.
- **Contrarian Play:** Kansas over Duke in Sweet 16 is our primary upset call precisely because of this injury.

### Jalen Wilson — UNC (5-seed, East) — Knee Injury
- **Status:** Out 4-6 weeks (definitively out for tournament)
- **Injury:** Torn meniscus suffered in late February
- **Impact Rating:** 8/10 — Wilson is UNC's best scorer
- **KenPom Adjustment:** UNC drops from #20 overall to approximately #30-35 without Wilson. More critically, their offensive rank drops from #12 to approximately #22.
- **Offensive Impact:** Wilson averages 20.4 PPG. RJ Davis is their next best at 20.4 PPG, but loses Wilson's post-up and mid-range creation.
- **Bracket Implication:** **VCU upset in Round of 64 is now a high-confidence call.** UNC at 80% offense vs VCU's #31 KenPom defense creates a genuine mismatch.
- **Note:** This transforms a chalk pick (UNC wins R64) into a contrarian opportunity. Most pools still have 10-12% on UNC.

### Ryan Huff — Gonzaga (3-seed, West) — Knee Injury
- **Status:** Out (season-ending injury confirmed)
- **Injury:** ACL/meniscus, suffered in WCC Tournament
- **Impact Rating:** 7/10 — Huff is Gonzaga's starting power forward and rim protector
- **KenPom Adjustment:** Gonzaga drops from #12 overall to approximately #20-22. More critically, their defensive rank drops from #28 to approximately #50-55.
- **Defensive Impact:** Huff averages 14.1 PPG and 7.8 RPG but his real value is as the team's interior anchor. Without him, Gonzaga gives up driving lanes.
- **Bracket Implication:** **BYU upset in Round of 32 is high-confidence.** BYU's Kevin Young system creates exactly the physical mismatches that expose a depleted Gonzaga frontcourt.
- **Note:** Gonzaga should still win Round of 64 vs a 14-seed on talent alone. But Round of 32 vs BYU is legitimately a coin flip with Huff out.
