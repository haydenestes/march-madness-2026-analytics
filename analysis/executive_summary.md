## Key Decisions That Differentiate This Bracket

### 1. Fade Duke — The Pool-Killer
Duke at 38% pool ownership means: if they win the title, almost everyone beats you. The risk-reward is terrible. Cooper Flagg's back injury makes them a **different team**. Kansas in the Sweet 16 is our upset call.

### 2. Michigan Over Auburn in Midwest
The Midwest bracket is the most exploitable. Michigan (#9 KenPom) has the softest Final Four path in the field. Auburn (#4 overall) faces Kansas/Duke-type teams early. Juwan Howard's system translates in March.

### 3. Houston Upsets Florida — The Marquee Upset
The Elite 8 South game. Houston's defense (#3 KenPom) is the best matchup problem for Florida's offense (#3 KenPom offense). Kelvin Sampson has been here before. Florida at #1 KenPom is real, but the matchup breaks the wrong way.

### 4. Roster Madness Value Picks
The 9/10/11 tier in Roster Madness is where equity is created. Iowa (9), Texas A&M (10), and VCU (11) all project to win their first-round games. These three picks alone could swing 20-30 points in pool scoring.
