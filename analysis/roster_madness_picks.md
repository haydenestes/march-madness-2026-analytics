## Picks by Tier

### Tier 1: Lock Picks (Seeds 1-3)
These are chalk — just pick the best statistical team at each seed.

**1-seed: Arizona**
No debate needed. Healthiest 1-seed with the cleanest path. If you want to get cute, Florida is the best 1-seed statistically, but Arizona is the best *pool pick* because of ownership and home region.

**2-seed: Purdue**
Over Michigan, Houston, and UConn because Purdue has the best offense in this tier (#2 nationally) and their path through the West bracket is more defined. Braden Smith is an elite point guard for tournament play.

**3-seed: Michigan State**
Over Gonzaga (injured), Baylor, and Saint Mary's. Tom Izzo at 3-seed is automatic. His 7-1 Elite 8 record is the best tournament coaching record in history. The #1 rebounding rate creates problems for every opponent.

---

### Tier 2: Value Picks (Seeds 4-6)
This is where pools are won. Don't autopick — find KenPom mismatches.

**4-seed: Nebraska over Texas Tech, Clemson, Tennessee**
Texas Tech is injured (Toppin). Clemson fails the KenPom filter vs Iowa. Tennessee is legitimate but Nebraska's defense (#6 KenPom) is the best among all 4-seeds. Hoiberg's system travels.

**5-seed: Wisconsin over UNC, Alabama, Maryland**
UNC is eliminated (Wilson out). Wisconsin is objectively the best 5-seed by KenPom at #16 overall. They're essentially a misseeded 3 or 4 seed. Auto-pick them.

**6-seed: Louisville over BYU, Illinois, Creighton**
Toughest call in Tier 2. BYU is our upset pick (Gonzaga game), so they're projected to win more games. But for Roster Madness, we're scoring based on seed performance. Louisville at #19 KenPom playing a 5/12 winner in Round of 32 is the best expected value at the 6-slot.

*Alternative: BYU if you believe strongly in the Gonzaga upset run lasting to Sweet 16.*

---

### Tier 3: Upset Picks (Seeds 9-11)
**This is where 20-30 points of differential value lives.**

**9-seed: Iowa — THE KEY PICK**
Clemson (#21 KenPom) vs Iowa (#32 KenPom) — Iowa is 11 spots below Clemson in KenPom but has better size, experience, and tournament coaching. This is the clearest statistical mismatch at the 8/9 game. 75% confidence. At 6% pool ownership on Iowa as the 9-pick, this is the highest-equity single decision in Roster Madness.

**10-seed: Texas A&M — HIGH VALUE**
Saint Mary's is a chalk pick here because they're the 7-seed. But Texas A&M has 9 seniors and scores 87.7 PPG — most in the entire field. The Gaels play slow (68.1 tempo) and rely on guards. Texas A&M's physical post play and tempo advantage is a genuine mismatch. 60% confidence, ~5% pool ownership.

**11-seed: VCU — NEAR-LOCK UPSET**
This is the closest thing to a free money pick in Roster Madness. UNC is missing Jalen Wilson (definitively out, torn meniscus). Without Wilson, UNC drops from #20 to ~#30 KenPom. VCU's full-court press at #47 KenPom — the best 11-seed by far — is a nightmare matchup for a shorthanded Carolina team. 60% confidence, only ~3% pool ownership.

---

### Tier 4: Chalice Picks (Seeds 12-13)
These are 50/50 games. Pick based on record and profile, not narrative.

**12-seed: High Point**
30-4 record is the best in the 12-seed tier. Three-point shooting creates spacing that neutralizes larger opponents. Standard 12/5 upset rate is ~35% — High Point projects above that average.

**13-seed: Hawaii**
The most "legitimate" 13-seed in the field. 27-6 record. Their star player (JoVon McClanahan) is a real Division 1 scorer. The travel burden for Nebraska flying to a neutral site in Hawaii's timezone creates a genuine edge. Still a long shot, but best option at this slot.

---

### Tier 5: Floor Picks (Seeds 14-16)
No strategic value — just pick the best cover chance and move on.

**14-seed: North Dakota State** — Sam Griesel is a real player. 27-7 record.
**15-seed: Queens NC** — Floor pick. No 15 goes deep. Minimal point exposure.
**16-seed: Siena** — Best MAAC team we've seen in years. Will keep it interesting.
