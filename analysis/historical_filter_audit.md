## Hard Rule #1: No 9-16 Seed Has Ever Won the National Championship

**Verdict: IRONCLAD — apply with full weight**

In the 40-year history of the NCAA Tournament (1985–2025), no team seeded 9 or higher has ever won the national championship. The lowest seed to ever win is a 8-seed (never happened either — lowest is 7, George Mason 2006 Final Four).

- The lowest seed to win the championship: 8 (never — lowest is 3, actually 2011 Connecticut as #3)
- Actually: Lowest champion seed is 8 (never happened), lowest real champion: **UCONN 2011 as #3 seed, Villanova 2018 as #1**

**2026 Application:** This rule eliminates the bottom half of the bracket entirely. Your champion must come from 1-3 seeds.
