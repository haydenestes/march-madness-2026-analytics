Framework

Analytical Methodology

📊

KenPom Efficiency Filters

Champion must rank top-20 in adjusted offensive efficiency and top-40 in defensive efficiency. Verified against 15 years of title winners with zero exceptions on the defense filter. Eliminates 22 of 30 teams immediately.

🩹

Injury Adjustments

Nine players carry significant injury or availability risk in 2026: Caleb Foster (Duke, fractured foot — out for season), Patrick Ngongba (Duke, foot soreness — questionable), Caleb Wilson (UNC, broken thumb — out for season), Braden Huff (Gonzaga, kneecap dislocation confirmed — out first two rounds), JT Toppin (Texas Tech, ACL — out for season), Richie Saunders (BYU, torn ACL — out for season), Aden Holloway (Alabama, suspended indefinitely following felony arrest Mar. 17 — will not play; second-leading scorer at 16.8 PPG / 43.8% 3P, leaving Alabama depleted on both offense and defense), Carter Welling (Clemson, torn ACL — out for season; top rebounder and second scorer gone, strengthening Iowa over Clemson upset pick further). Each materially impacts KenPom efficiency projections.

🎮

Pool Ownership Game Theory

Winning a bracket pool requires separating from the field at key inflection points. Duke at 38% ownership with an injury is the worst equity pick in the field. Arizona at 14% with equivalent talent is the highest-equity champion selection.

📈

Bracket Path Mapping

Michigan draws the easiest projected path to the Final Four in the Midwest. Alabama (#4 seed) enters the tournament without Aden Holloway (suspended indefinitely, Mar. 17), making Michigan's Sweet 16 matchup near-certain to advance. Arizona's West bracket avoids the toughest 2-seeds. Path difficulty is weighted into every deep-run projection and upset confidence score.

Core Principles

  1. 1Separate "best team" from "best bracket pick" — pool equity is the objective, not accuracy.
  2. 2Champion must pass KenPom top-20 offense and top-40 defense — a 40-year hard rule with zero exceptions on defense.
  3. 3Never pick a 9–16 seed as champion — ironclad over 40 years of tournament history.
  4. 4Fade heavily-owned teams with injury exposure. Duke at 38% public ownership + Caleb Foster out (fractured foot) = worst risk-adjusted pick in the field.
  5. 5Experience beats talent in March. Senior-led teams outperform their seedings. Weight accordingly.

Key T-Rank Findings

🏆 Vanderbilt #10 T-Rank — Massively Underseeded

Seeded 5th, Vanderbilt profiles as a top-10 team nationally by T-Rank — statistically tougher than Nebraska (#14 KenPom). Any bracket picking Nebraska deep should reconsider; Vanderbilt is a genuine sleeper threat in the Round of 32 and beyond.

🚨 Illinois TORD 365th — Houston Will Feast

Illinois ranks dead last nationally in turnover rate forced (TORD). Houston's defense is built entirely around generating turnovers and capitalizing on possessions. This is a catastrophic stylistic mismatch — fade Illinois in any bracket matchup against Houston.

⚠️ Nebraska FTR 350th — Free Throw Vulnerability

Nebraska's free throw rate (FTR) ranks 350th nationally — they almost never get to the line. In close tournament games decided by fouls and late free throws, this is a meaningful liability. Temper deep-run expectations accordingly.

🎯 Akron EFG% 7th Nationally (58.5%) vs Texas Tech

Akron shoots 58.5% effective field goal percentage — 7th best in the entire country. Texas Tech's defense has collapsed late in the season. This is a dangerous first-round matchup that most brackets will overlook entirely.

🔍 Gonzaga Still #11 T-Rank Without Huff

Despite Braden Huff's kneecap dislocation (out first two rounds), Gonzaga remains the #11 team in the country by T-Rank with a top-10 defense. BYU at 75% upset confidence may be slightly overconfident — Gonzaga has the talent to win without him.

✅ Iowa #26 T-Rank — Best 9-Seed Value Confirmed

Iowa's T-Rank of #26 sits 11 spots above Clemson's #30 (now further weakened by Carter Welling's torn ACL). T-Rank corroborates the KenPom gap — Iowa over Clemson is the highest-confidence upset call in the bracket, now at 85%.

Arizona
1-Seed · West Region
T-Rank #3KenPom #3

The Wildcats beat 12 ranked teams this season and have given up only one Kill Shot all year per EvanMiya. The healthiest and most balanced 1-seed, underowned in public pools at 14% vs Duke's 38%.

Tournament Ceiling: National Champion
Michigan
1-Seed · Midwest Region
T-Rank #2KenPom #2

Michigan's frontcourt is historically dominant — Lendeborg (6-9), Johnson Jr. (6-9), and Mara (7-3) form one of the tallest, most skilled front lines in college basketball history. The Wolverines shoot 59% inside the arc and hold opponents to 50% on the same shots — a 15-point delta that is statistically extraordinary. Lottery pick Lendeborg averages 14.3 PPG / 7.3 RPG while anchoring the nation's top interior defense. Michigan lost just two regular season games by a combined 8 points and enters as the slight betting favorite.

Tournament Ceiling: National Championship Game

Silas Demary Jr. leads in assists and steals, and a battle-hardened path through the brutal East bracket makes them the most proven team at the Final Four.

Tournament Ceiling: National Championship Game

Elite defense ranked #3 nationally in turnover rate forced.
